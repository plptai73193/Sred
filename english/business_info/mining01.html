Mining Operations
Contents of Business

The Hishikari Mine*, Japan's largest gold mine, currently operates on a commercial scale. SRED has engaged in development and exploration work there since being contracted by Sumitomo Metal Mining (SMM) in 1983 when the mine opened.

While cultivating technology, implementing thorough safety management, and properly responding to daily changing situations at the mine operations site, SRED contributes to stable operations.


Development construction

At the Hishikari Mine, we engage in tunneling, mineral extraction, and other mine development construction operations.In mine development construction, the bedrock strength, mineral vein direction, and ore quality differ with each tunnel. However, with the technology and know-how we have cultivated up to now, we are able to contribute to safe and stable mine development.

Load-haul-dump (LHD) loaders, "drill jumbos," and other special mine-specification heavy machinery enable us to perform the construction work described below.

  • Tunneling

    Work that involves tunnel excavation for the purpose of exploration, development, drainage, and/or ventilation is called "tunneling," while work that involves excavating in a certain direction, particularly along a vein, is called "drift tunneling". The work is performed in the following order:


    • Boring: A hole is opened in order to load explosives for breaking up bedrock.
    • Loading/blasting explosives: Explosives are placed in the hole and set off to break up bedrock.
    • Loading/transporting material: Blasted/broken rock is loaded in heavy transport equipment and transported above ground.
    • Installing support: Bedrock exposed by blasting is supported by sprayed concrete and/or steel framing to reinforce the tunnel.

    Repeating these tasks, development proceeds at an average pace of about 150 meters per month.

  • Extraction

    Ore is mined between the top and bottom of a drift tunnel excavated along a vein at each level (depth intervals of 30 to 10 meters), while blasting is carried out between top and bottom. The work is performed in the following order:


    • Boring: A hole is opened vertically upward/downward in order to load explosives for breaking up bedrock.
    • Loading/blasting explosives: Explosives are placed in the hole and set off to break up bedrock vertically.
    • Loading/hauling material: Blasted/broken mineral ore is loaded in heavy transport equipment and hauled above ground.
    • Backfilling: Spaces where ore was removed are backfilled with unneeded rock (called muck) to return the area to its condition prior to blasting.

    Repeating these tasks, development proceeds at an average pace of about 40 meters per month.
    Ore hauled above ground during tunneling and mineral extraction operations is separated by Sumitomo Metal Mining (SMM) and then transported to a refinery, where the gold is refined.

    We strive to hand down our mining techniques so that these skills won't be lost.
