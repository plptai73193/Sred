Laboratory Test

SRED conducts laboratory tests on geological samples such as rocks, minerals, soils, ores, etc. in collaboration with domestic and overseas laboratories.
Please contact us for inquiry and quotation to the following e-mail address. bunseki@sred.co.jp

Chemical analysis

By using analytical instruments such as X-ray fluorescence spectroscopy (XRF), inductively coupled plasma mass spectrometer (ICP-MS) and inductively coupled plasma emission spectrometer (ICP-AES), the major and trace components (gold, silver, rare metals, rare earth elements, etc.) and the concentration of isotopic composition are measured. In addition, qualitative analysis and surface analysis (element distribution concentration) of elements on the sample surface by electron probe micro analyzer (EPMA) and radiometric dating (K-Ar method, U-Pb method, Rb-Sr method, etc.) are conducted.

Microscopic observation

SRED prepares specimens for microscopic observation of rocks and ores and observe to identify mineral composition and texture by polarizing microscope. It is effective in determining rock types and identifying minerals contained in ores.

Polarizing microscopic photo of rock thin section

Reflecting microscopic photo of ore

X-ray diffraction

X-rays are irradiated to samples which are finely pulverized and minerals contained in samples are identified from the measured X-ray diffraction chart. It is effective to identify clay minerals contained in altered rocks in the exploration of mineral resources and geothermal resources.

X-ray diffraction chart of altered clay in geothermal field

Physical property test of rocks

SRED measures resistivity, charging rate and magnetic susceptibility of rocks by equipment shown in photos. These tests are used to evaluate the amount of metal minerals and the strength of alteration of rocks in the exploration of mineral resources and geothermal resources.

Measuring resistivity and charging rate of rock

Microfossils observation

Relative age and sedimentary environment can be estimated by identifying types of foraminifera, radiolarians and diatoms contained in rocks and ocean floor sediments.

Table of relative age of foraminifera and nannofossil

Mode measurement of rock thin section

The content (area ratio) of each mineral in rock is determined by mode measurement under microscopic observation of rock thin section.

Fluid inclusion measurement

By examining the trapped fluid when rocks and ores were formed, it is possible to know the temperature, pressure, fluid concentration, etc. when the fluid was taken in minerals.
