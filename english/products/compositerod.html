Composite rods

A composite rod is composed of tungsten carbide tips brazed with nickel-silver. Tungsten carbide of the toughness metal keeps hard in high temperature and has superior abrasion resistane. It is applied to hard surfacing of top blades of steel pipes drilling in the earth and cement concrete and also buckets of construction machines. Metal parts hard surfaced by tungsten carbide cotribute to cost down due to improvement of work efficient and extention of machine life.

Superior abrasion resistance maximized by tight welding with base metal

Heating base metal up to high temperature makes brazing filler metal melt of overlay rod into base metal fully, tungsten carbide tips are tight welded with base metal. High surface temparature of base metal beyond 900 degrees Celsius is suitable for melting brazing filler metal of our composite rod and toughen adherence of tungsten carbide tips. Substrate treatment of apply flux to heated base metal minimizes oxidation of materials and improve final mechanical quality. This treatment keeps high drilling and cutting performance and abrasion resistance for a long time.

Overlay rod

An overlay rod is the brazing metal for welding a composite rod of a base metal of a steel pipe and a steel rod. Melting overlay rod and flux into the base metal before welding composite rod, fixing of tungsten crabide tips in welding strengthen. In the case of the big gaps between the tips, use of an overlay rod is strongly recommended in order to avoid peel off of the tips.

Minimum oredered number is an overlay rod. The products can be shipped in the ordered day. You can purchase the required number whn needed.

Flux

Flux is anitioxidant in welding a composite rod. used for welding a composite rod to base metal with an overlay rod. In heating the base metal before welding, flux removes oxide filmes form the surface of the base metal and avoid oxidation of the base metal. Cosequently, mechanical strength of welding increase.

Flux poweder is packed in a metal cann at the net weight of 50, 100, 200 and 500 g.

Q&A

  • How long orederderd commodities are delivered?

  • Are composite rods sold loose?
    Yes. Our all types of composite rod are sold from minimum 1 rod.
  • Are a overlay rod and flux necessary for welding comsiterod to base metal?
    Yes, reccomende. A overlay rod melts into base metal and mount tugstentips on base metal tightly. Stronger chemical affinity of flux with oxicide film on base metal, oxygen and nitrogen in the air than heated metal prevent oxidation and mechanical deterioration of heated base metal.
  • Does flux remove rusts, coatings and greases on the base metal?
    No. Because flux cannnot remove them, they should be fully removed before welding work.

  • How many degrees are base metal heated whn welding?

  • Can a composite rod apply to the top blades of bits of percussiopn drill?
    No, we don't reccommend it. It is highly possible that shocks in vaibration drilling break tungsten carbide tips.
